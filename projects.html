Selected Personal Projects (for fun)

  • Secret Santa Organizer
    www.secret-santa-organizer.com
    Planning a secret santa? Do it here. Easier than pulling names from a hat. Great for co-workers, friends, or family.
    Janice and I worked on this project together in 2009. I'd been organizing secret santa gift exchanges for my friends for a few years and had a script laying around to do the randomizing and email sending. Then Janice and I thought we should build a full webapp so that other people can use it too.
  • RIPE: Real Estate Investment Evaluator
    www.edwardsshieh.com
    RIPE helps you identify profitable real estate.
    Ken and I started a real estate investment company in 2008 and started making sophisticated spreadsheets to evaluate potential properties for acquisition. It turns out that a lot of the existing spreadsheets on the market weren't comprehensive enough so we sought to build one that included every metric an investor might look at including taxes and the time value of money to name a few. We decided to put it online and see if anybody wanted to buy it and we've sold far more than we initally expected.
  • iPhone App: Tennis Score Keeper
    http://itunes.apple.com/app/tennis-score-keeper-basic/id360540565?mt=8#
    Tennis Score Keeper Basic tracks the score so you can concentrate on the game. Wear it on your arm and press one button after each point or give it to a friend and make sure the score is right. Never forget the score again.
    In 2010, my mom, who plays tennis competitively almost every single day in various tournaments and leagues, told me she was having a hard time keeping track of the scores of matches and that a lot of her friends had the same problem. It causes disputes and can be a problem in high-stakes matches. I used it as an opportunity to learn the iPhone sdk and wrote this app mostly for fun.
